pH Level
?
pH is the master variable of pool chemistry. Low pH (acidic) corrodes equipment, irritates eyes and skin, and destroys chlorine effectiveness. High pH (basic) causes cloudy water, scale deposits, and dramatically reduces chlorine sanitisation. Target: exactly 7.2. Formula: (difference from 7.2) × 30 × (pool size in thousands of litres).

Free Chlorine
?
Free chlorine is your primary sanitiser, killing bacteria, algae, and pathogens. Ideal range: 2–4 ppm. If above 4, production is reduced proportionally. Example: if chlorine is 8 (double the max of 4) and production is at 4 hrs, reduce to 2 hrs. Below 2 ppm, dose up to the midpoint of 3 ppm.

Total Alkalinity
?
Total Alkalinity (TA) buffers your pH against rapid swings. Target: 120 ppm (acceptable range 80–120). Formula: (120 − TA) × 15 × (pool size ÷ 10,000). Example: TA is 80, pool is 40,000 L → 40 × 15 × 4 = 2,400 g (2.4 kg) of sodium bicarbonate.

Stabiliser (CYA)
?
Cyanuric Acid (CYA) protects chlorine from UV sunlight. Ideal range: 30–50 ppm. For pools ≤30,000L: below 25 (but above 13) add 0.5 kg; below 13 add 1 kg. For pools >30,000L: if below 30, add 1 kg regardless of how far below. Reduction only possible via dilution (partial drain).

Calcium Hardness
?
Calcium Hardness (CH) measures dissolved calcium. Low CH makes water aggressive — it leaches calcium from plaster and grout. High CH causes scale. Ideal: 200–400 ppm. Reduce only by dilution.

Phosphates
?
Phosphates fuel algae growth. If the reading is 500 ppb or above, add 100 mL of phosphate remover. Below 500 ppb, no treatment is required unless algae is persistent. Target: below 200 ppb.

Clarifier
?
Clarifier is a coagulant (typically polyquat or aluminium sulphate) that binds fine suspended particles together so the filter can capture them. Use weekly for maintenance or after heavy bather loads, storms, or algae treatments. Do not overdose — excess clarifier can cloud the water further.

Salt Level
?
Target salt level: 4,000 ppm. For every 1,000 units below 4,000, add one 20 kg salt bag per 20,000 L of pool water. Example: reading is 3,000 (1,000 short) with a 20,000 L pool → 1 bag. A 40,000 L pool → 2 bags. Use pool-grade NaCl (99%+ pure) only.
